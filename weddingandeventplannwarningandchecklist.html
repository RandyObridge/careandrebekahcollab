Here's What Nobody Tells You

After working with hundreds of couples and businesses, we've seen these same costly mistakes destroy events—and budgets—over and over again.

01

The "Ghost Planner" Trap

You hire a planner who looks great on Instagram... then they disappear for weeks at a time, take unannounced vacations right before your event, or delegate everything to inexperienced assistants who've never even met you.

02

The "Day-Of Surprise" Trap

Your "full-service" planner shows up on wedding day with ONE assistant when you expected three. Suddenly your family is setting up centerpieces and moving chairs while you're trying to get ready.

03

The "Wikipedia Expert" Trap

You ask for church recommendations and get a link to Google. You ask for unique venue ideas and get the same tired spots everyone uses. You're essentially paying thousands for someone to do what you could've Googled yourself.

04

The "Pretty Portfolio" Trap

Their website shows Martha Stewart-worthy events, but when it's YOUR day, they're texting in the corner while your timeline falls apart. Beautiful photos don't mean they can actually manage an event.

05

The "Vague Contract" Trap

You sign a contract that says "full planning" but doesn't specify what that includes. Two weeks before your event, every little request becomes an "additional fee." That $3,000 package just became $5,500.

06

The "Missing Timeline" Trap

No one enforces the schedule. Cocktail hour runs 30 minutes late. The first dance gets skipped. Your photographer leaves before cake cutting because someone didn't manage the vendors.

07

The "One Size Fits All" Trap

They don't understand the difference between decor needs and planning needs. You need both a visual designer AND someone to manage logistics, but you're paying for one person trying to do both jobs poorly.
